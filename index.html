They Feynman Lecturs on Physics

  • In 1961 Dr. Richard Feynman was given a lecturer position at Caltech where he taugh a two year introductory physics course for undergraduate students. They Feynman lectures on physics are a collection of his notes that Dr. Feyman wrote on the fly for the course.
  • The topics covered in these books range from Classical Mechanics, Electrodynamics, Quantum Mechanics, Special Relativity, and Statistical Mechanics. Dr. Feynmans unique teaching style has reached millions of listeners and has enlightened thousands.
  • This series is one of the best supplemental material any undergraduate student in physics could use.

Superfluidity

  • When teachnig at Caltech, Feynman offered a quantum mechanical explanation to the Russian Physicist Landau's Theory of Superfluidity.
  • Superconductivity has been noted ever since Faraday, but the theory had eluded physicists until Landau and others worked our the theormodynamics of the phenomenon. Feynman worked on Landaus theory by introducting quantum mechanics to explain the superconductivity and superfluidity peroperties of materials. Feynman theory was later completed and replaced by the now famous BCS Theory (Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer)
  • Superconductivity is a special property that presents itself in very cold conductors which lose all electrical resistance and become perfect conductors. Another interesting effects is the penetration of magnetic fields through the material and the obsolution of Lens Law.

Nobel Prize and Quantum Electrodynamics

  • Richard Feynman shared his Nobel Prize in Physics with Sin-Itiro Tomonaga and Julian Schiwnger for their fundamental work in quantum electrodynamics with deep-ploughing consequences for the physics of elementary particles. The prize was won in 1965 and signfied great achievement for Dr. Feynman.
  • QED is the relativistic quantum field theory of electrodynamics. It describes how light and matter interact and is the first theory that is in full agreement with special relativity and quantum mechanics!
  • QED can be described as a perturbation theory of the electromagnetic quantum vacuum. Feynman called it "the jewel of physics" for its accurate predictions of quantities like the anomalous magnetic moment of the electron and the Lamb shift of the energy levels of hydrogen. From QED we obtain the Feynman Diagrams.

Feynman Diagrams

  • Feynman Diagrams are diagrams that describe the subatomic particle interactions with high accuracy. It was invented by Feynman in 1948. These diagrams give a simple visualization of what would be a conglomeration of complicated equations and interactions.
